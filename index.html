Public Signals

The Problem.

This case study begins from public discussions around priority support, elderly traveller concerns, pickup friction, and clearer airport movement.
Insights were drawn from LinkedIn, keeping all identities anonymous.

A direct public request for a visible senior-friendly pathway on a trending BIAL post.

Arrival, pickup, and last-mile movement continue to be high-friction moments for elderly travellers.

Passengers are calling for better-designed experiences that feel easier, clearer, and more dignified.

Design Gap

Where the gap sits.

Bangalore Airport has a strong accessibility foundation through Passenger with Reduced Mobility (PRM) support, wheelchair assistance, Hidden Disabilities support, and inclusive passenger services.

However, many elderly travellers don't identify as PRM. They are mobile and independent, but still experience airport anxiety, speed pressure, standing fatigue, digital confusion, and the fear of slowing others down.

Journey Friction

Transition points are always the hardest.

For elderly travellers, airport friction often appears at transition points: the moments where they need to decode what happens next.

Opportunity Statement

How might Bangalore reduce uncertainty, effort, and pressure for elderly travellers across key airport transitions?

Opportunity Direction

Designing age-friendly airport journeys.

The following concepts explore how service, operational, and digital interventions could improve the airport experience across key points.

01

Senior Lane

A dedicated opt-in route through security and check-in for passengers 65 and above who are mobile but need more time, clearer guidance, and a lower-pressure environment.

02

Reimagined Security Screening

Security screening is the highest-friction point in the journey. At several international airports, secondary physical screening is only triggered when the body scanner alerts, removing default pat-down checks. Millimeter-wave scanners can further streamline screening, helping passengers move faster with less physical and cognitive effort while maintaining security standards.

03

BLR Pulse: Elderly Journey Mode

A simplified journey view within the existing BLR Pulse app that shows elderly passengers their next essential step at any point in the airport, along with pre-booked security slots, nearest rest stops, and real-time gate updates.

04

Companion Access Pass

A controlled, policy-led framework that allows one family member to accompany an elderly passenger through key transition points where operationally feasible.

05

Pause Points

Small, purposefully placed rest zones at major transition points across the terminal, specifically between check-in and security and between security and the gate. Each pause point includes seating, water access, large-text wayfinding panels, and visible staff presence.

Business + Brand Value

Ripple Effects.

Age-friendly passenger experience can become a long-term differentiator for airports shaping the future of travel, and position Bangalore as India’s benchmark for age-friendly airport experience.

Less anxiety

Calmer passenger movement through high-stress transitions.

Smoother flow

Reduced confusion at security, transfers, and arrival pickup.

Inclusive by design

Designing for one group often improves experiences for many others who are overlooked by standard systems.

More engagement

Comfortable passengers are more likely to use retail, F&B, and services.

Global recognition

A visible leadership position in inclusive airport design.

Closing Thought

Bangalore has a strong accessibility foundation. The next opportunity is: age-friendly passenger journeys.
